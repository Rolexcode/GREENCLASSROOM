The Growing Threat

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns ‚Äî largely driven by human activities, especially the burning of fossil fuels.
  • Sea levels are projected to rise over 1 meter by 2100.
  • Over 1 million species are at risk of extinction due to climate-related factors.
Melting Ice Caps: Polar ice is melting at unprecedented rates, contributing to rising sea levels and threatening coastal communities.

Powering the Future

Renewable energy sources like wind and solar offer sustainable alternatives to fossil fuels, helping reduce pollution and greenhouse gases.
  • Solar power can reduce household electricity costs by up to 70%.
  • Wind turbines now generate electricity for over 80 countries.
Wind Power: Harnesses the wind to produce electricity, providing a sustainable and eco-friendly energy alternative.

Losing Our Lungs

Forests are being destroyed at alarming rates, impacting climate, biodiversity, and indigenous communities who rely on them.
  • The Amazon loses over 10,000 square kilometers of forest yearly.
  • 20% of the world's oxygen comes from rainforests.
Loss of Forests: Trees are cut down for agriculture, fuel, or industry, reducing biodiversity and increasing carbon dioxide in the atmosphere.

Everyday Actions That Matter

Simple daily practices like reducing waste, recycling, and conserving water can make a lasting positive impact on the planet.
  • Turning off lights when not in use can save 20% energy yearly.
  • Carrying a reusable bottle avoids ~1,500 plastic bottles per person per year.
Recycling & Reuse: Recycling reduces waste and conserves natural resources. Reusing items also cuts down pollution.
